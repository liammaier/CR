Thinking About The Question

What happens to a living plant throughout the day and night?

How does the 24-hour day/night cycle affect the release of water vapor by plants?

The turning of the earth on its axis causes a regular and predictable daily cycle of light and darkness. Animals, plants and even microbes have built-in timepieces that help them know when to eat, when to rest, and when to wake up.

How does the day differ from the night? Think about differences in light, temperature, humidity, and other things that might matter to a plant.

Trial 2 - 24-hour cycle of bagged leaves

  1. Think about the stomata openings we learned about in Investigation 1. The stomata are the plant's 'lungs'. It uses them to breathe and exchange gases like we do. They are most numerous on the underside of the leaf. You would need a microscope to see them.

    Guard cells surround the stomata openings. In light the guard cells swell, causing the pore to open. In the dark or under drought conditions the guard cells close the stomata and no photosynthesis takes place. Opening of the stomata allows CO2 to move and spread into the leaf, and it also allows water vapor to move out of the leaf. The release of water from leaves through their stomata is called transpiration.

    What different conditions will cause the stomata to open or change size?

    essay answer:

  2. Now that you have thought about your experimental setup for observing when a leaf is transpiring, prepare for your 24-hour run. Ask your teacher to help with the computer settings so that the computer will never go to sleep. Make sure that the room lights will not stay on all night!

Refer to Technical Hint: Connecting the Vernier relative humidity sensor to connect the relative humidity sensor.
