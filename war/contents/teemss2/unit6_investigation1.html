Unit 6 - Water and air temperature - Investigation - 1 - Temperature of mixing water

Mix fluids and measure temperature changes

This unit addresses NSES standards for inquiry and physical science at grades 5-8 and NCTM standards on Algebra and Data Analysis at grades 6-8.

sensors: temperature

Discovery Question: How do I mix water in a fish tank to adjust the temperature?

Students use a temperature sensor to compare the feeling of temperature with the result of a measurement. They investigate still air, moving air, and water temperatures.

Investigation Introduction

Discovery Question: How do I mix water in a fish tank to adjust the temperature?

In this activity, you will investigate how the temperature changes when two cups of water at different temperatures are mixed.

Materials

  • Refer to Technical Hint: Vernier temperature sensor with Go!Link
  • 3 large foam cups (500 ml or 16 oz)
  • 1 small foam cup (250 ml or 8 oz)
  • 2 empty 35 mm film canisters
  • ice water
  • warm water - less than 50 degrees Celsius (or 120 degrees Fahrenheit)
  • large pitchers or jugs
4

Safety

The warm water in this investigation cannot exceed 50 degrees Celsius (or 120 degrees Fahrenheit). If the water temperature exceeds this temperature, burns may result.

Further Investigations

  • Can you come up with an equation that could help someone else accurately predict the final temperature for three different volumes of water mixing, while only knowing the initial temperatures and volumes?
  • You have a bathtub that has 60 liters of 30 degrees Celsius water in it and you want to bring the temperature up to 40 degrees Celsius. What is the least number of readings you can make with the temperature sensor to get your desired final temperature? Note: you can try this with a bathtub at home, or do it on a smaller scale using cups of water in the classroom.
