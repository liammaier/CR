How do we make sounds and how do we hear sounds?

Sounds come from things that vibrate. A vibration is something moving back and forth in a regular way. Some things vibrate and make sounds that we can hear. All musical instruments have some part of them that vibrates and makes the musical sound.

You can see how sounds are vibrations by building a model. Build a balloon drum by stretching a balloon over one end of a tin can that has both ends open. Working together, stretch the balloon tight and hold it in place by putting a rubber band around the edge.

Talk into the open bottom of the tin can while touching the balloon.

Most laptops and Macs have a built-in microphone. If there is no built-in microphone, you need to connect an external microphone. You can use an inexpensive lapel microphone with a microphone jack, or a more expensive one with a USB connection. Directions for attaching either type of microphone are shown below.

To connect a microphone jack microphone, connect the jack to the microphone port on your computer. The microphone port is usually colored pink.

To connect a USB microphone, plug the USB connector into the USB port on your computer.

Further Investigations

  • Build a megaphone with paper and use it to make your voice louder. Also try to listen with it. Explain how you think it works.

  • Most sounds have many frequencies in them, and not just one, even though they sound like one pitch. The Sound Grapher has a special "Frequency Grapher" feature to display the frequencies that are present in simple and complicated sounds. Refer to Technical Hint: Frequency Grapher to use the Frequency Grapher.

    Use the Frequency Grapher to look at the mix of different frequencies that are in different musical instruments.

  • Explore how air can make something vibrate, as it does with the vocal cords. For example, let air out of a balloon. Pinch it tight to make a pure sound. Or try blowing across a soda bottle top.
