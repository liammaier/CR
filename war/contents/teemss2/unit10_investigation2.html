Thinking About The Question

Can you design a greenhouse to maintain a good environment for different plants?

Over the centuries, people have developed many plants as sources of food. Farmers continuously select new varieties that are more productive, tastier, more resistant to diseases, or more suitable to the market in one way or another. Each species has its unique personality. It prefers particular conditions that the farmer must provide in order to get the best crop.

For example, tomatoes and cucumbers love warm temperatures and lots of sunshine throughout their growing season. They also need continuous watering for best fruit production. By contrast, lettuce is a cool-weather crop. It does well in spring and fall. If temperatures are too hot, it will "bolt," which means it makes a hard stalk and has bitter leaves. It should be in the shade in hot weather.
