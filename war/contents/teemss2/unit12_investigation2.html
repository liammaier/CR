Unit 12 - Motion - Investigation - 2 - Displaying changing velocity

Graph, describe, and duplicate motion

This unit addresses NSES standards for physical science and inquiry at grades 5-8 and NCTM standards on Algebra and Data Analysis at grades 6-8.

sensors: motion

Discovery Question: When am I changing velocity or accelerating?

Students observe already existing motion graphs and describe the motions needed to make the graph and test their descriptions by reproducing the original graphs while using a motion sensor. The students calculate velocity at various locations on motion graphs and explore the relationship between velocity and acceleration while tracking their own motion.

  5. Why would changing velocity, otherwise known as acceleration, be shown as a curve on the graph? The black lines, called tangents, meet the curve at a single point and show the slope of the curve, and hence the velocity, at that point. Notice that if the line is curved, the slopes of the tangents are constantly changing. Hence the velocities are changing as well.

Investigation Technical Hints

Technical Hint - Learning how the motion sensor works

Bats fly back and forth in a cave at night to find their food. While flying at night, a bat emits squeaks that reflect off the walls of caves and flying insects. These squeaks return to the bat's nervous system and are used as clues to determine the location and shape of the objects. The bat can tell where the next meal is and when to avoid other objects.

A motion sensor (also called an ultrasonic detector) uses technology for a similar purpose. A motion sensor uses a motion detector that sends out high frequency sound waves to a target object and waits for the sound wave to come back. The motion sensor measures the time needed for the wave to leave and return to the detector. What is so unique about a motion sensor is that software can determine and display the distance a wave covered based on the speed of sound in a graph.

Technical Hint - Setting up a walking track

To set up a walking track, follow the steps listed below:

  1. Find an open space around 1 meter wide and 5 meters long (possibly in a hallway).
  2. Using masking tape, mark off the straight-line distance with half-meter divisions, starting at the motion sensor.
  3. Place the motion sensor on a stool or table that is waist high. Align the motion sensor with the tape.
