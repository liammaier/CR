Thinking About The Question

How can you extract water from the air?

As you discovered in Investigation 1, much of the water cycle is visible, both in the form of liquid water (oceans, rivers, lakes, precipitation such as rain) and in the form of solid water (ice or snow, precipitation such as falling snow or hail). However, a fraction of the water in the water cycle is invisible in that it is held as a gas in the air. This invisible amount of water accounts for about 0.001% of all water on earth. What do you think would happen to the water cycle if this "invisible" water did not exist? Get together with your group members and discuss this question. Write your thoughts below.

essay answer:
