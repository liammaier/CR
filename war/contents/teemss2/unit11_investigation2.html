Trial 3 - How much can you exhale?

  1. When you increase the size of your chest cavity, the air pressure in your chest cavity goes down. It turns out that for any fixed amount of air, the relationship between pressure (P) and volume (V) is defined as P1V1 = P2V2. The "1" stands for initial values and the "2" stands for final values. This relationship is called Boyle's Law. Using Boyle's Law, can you calculate what the air pressure in the chest cavity is in situation 2 below?
