The Notes app can be used to keep track of short-term or long-term notes (e.g., the medications you take or notes from a meeting that you want to keep). Figure 10-1 shows the New Note screen.

To Create a New Note

  • Tap the Notes app on the Home screen.
  • Tap in the upper right corner.
  • Enter your new note.
  • Tap in the upper right corner. [Note: The iPad does not require you to tap Done.]
  • To return to the Notes screen (the list of notes you have created thus far), tap Notes in the upper left corner.

To Open or Edit an Existing Note

  • On the Notes screen, tap the title of the note you would like to open or edit.
  • Tap the screen to bring up the keyboard to edit.
  • Edit the note as desired.
  • Tap in the upper right corner. [Note: The iPad saves notes automatically and does not require a Done button.]
  • Tap Notes in the upper left corner to return to the Notes screen (list of notes).

Siri Basics

Siri allows you to create, search, or add to existing notes in the Notes app by speaking. To interact with the Contacts app using Siri:

  1. Press and hold the Home Button until “What can I help you with?” and the microphone appear on the screen. Tap the microphone to turn it on if it is not blinking.
  2. Siri will understand a number of verbal commands, such as:
    1. Note that I bought lunch at Ruby’s.
    2. Note to follow up with Jan.
    3. Search for Meds note.
