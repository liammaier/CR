Chapter 16: The App Store, iTunes, iBooks, and Newsstand

The App Store is typically where you will go to purchase, download, and install new apps. Before you can purchase apps, you will be asked to enter your Apple ID account information. Purchases made through the App Store, Newsstand, iBooks, etc. are automatically charged to the credit card on file and associated with your Apple ID. Downloading free apps still requires an Apple ID account.

To Find an App at the App Store

  • Tap the App Store icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap one of the tabs at the top or bottom of the App Store screen to find apps you may be interested in.
  • Enter Search keyword(s) if necessary.
  • Tap the app reference to view app information.
  • Review the app information including description, sample screen shots, price, and overall rating before purchasing.

To Update Apps Purchased at the App Store

  • Tap the Updates tab in the lower right corner.
  • Review the apps that have updates available.
  • Tap Update All in upper right corner or tap the individual app to bring up the Info screen for that app and tap Update.
  • Enter your Apple ID password. The updates will download and install automatically.

iTunes: Music & Videos

You can purchase music and videos directly from your device via the iTunes app. There are several options at the bottom of the iTunes screen to help you find the music or videos you are looking for: Music, Videos, Search, etc. The buttons at the top of the screen change according to which of those options you selected. For example, Figure 16-4 shows the Music tab of iTunes. The options at the top of the Music tab are New Releases, Top Tens, and Genres.

Similarly, the Videos tab provides the options of Movies, TV Shows, and Music Videos. The Search tab provides—you guessed it—a search field to search for an item by name or keyword(s).

The Purchased tab lets you view your prior iTunes purchases. Finally, the More tab lets you explore several other categories, including ringtones, podcasts, and audiobooks among other things.

To Purchase Music & Videos from iTunes

  • Tap the iTunes icon on the Home screen.
  • Find music and videos via the Music, Videos, or Search buttons at the bottom of the screen. Enter search keyword(s) if necessary.
  • Once you obtain a listing of possibilities from any of the above-mentioned methods, tap a music or video reference to view its information.
  • Review the music or video information including description, overall rating, etc. before purchasing.
  • Tap the title of a song reference to hear a sample of the song track. Tap the Preview option to see a trailer of a selected movie/video.
  • Tap the album price to purchase the entire album or tap the price next to each track to purchase just that track. Tap the option to either rent or buy the movie/video.
  • Enter your Apple ID password. The music or video will automatically download.

iBooks

You can purchase electronic books (books you can read on your device) via the iBooks app.

To Purchase Books

  • Tap the iBooks icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Store icon in the upper right corner.
  • Use the tabs at the top and bottom of the screen to help find the desired book.
  • Tap the book reference.
  • Review the book information including description, overall rating, price, etc. before purchasing.
  • Tap the book price to purchase the book or tap the Get Sample option to download a sample of the book.
  • Enter your Apple ID password. The book will automatically download.

Newsstand

You can purchase magazine and newspaper subscriptions and single editions directly from your device via the Newsstand app.

To Purchase from Newsstand

  • Tap the Newsstand icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Store icon in the upper right corner (see Figure 16-7).
  • Use the tabs at the bottom of the screen to help find the desired magazine or newspaper.
  • Tap the magazine or newspaper reference.
  • Review the information including description, overall rating, price, etc. before purchasing.
  • Tap the magazine or newspaper price to install the app associated with your selection. Watch out; although many of the prices are listed as free, the app is free, but the editions of the magazine or newspaper are not always free. You have to download the app to see purchase options and amounts.
  • Enter your Apple ID password. The app will automatically download.
