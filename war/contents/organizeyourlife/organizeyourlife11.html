Chapter 11: The Voice Memo App

The Voice Memo app turns your device into a digital recorder. You can use the digital recorder for any number of things, e.g.:

  • Record information from the radio (e.g., a phone number regarding a product or activity).
  • Record important information from a doctor appointment.
  • Record a meeting.
  • Record your child’s first words.

Figure 11-1 shows the Voice Memo app Mic/Recording screen.

The red button icon to the left is the Record button. Simply tap the Record button to being recording. The List button on the right brings up a list of all recordings. When a recording starts (as shown in Figure 11-2), the Record button turns into a Pause button and the List button turns into a Stop button .

To Create a New Recording

  • Tap the Voice Memos app on the Home screen (it may be in the Utilities folder).
  • Tap the Record button in the lower left corner.
  • Begin speaking.
  • Tap Pause button in the lower left corner to temporarily pause the recording or tap the Stop button on the lower right corner to end the recording.

After recording, you will want to find and play back the voice memo.

To Find and Listen to Previous Recordings

  • Tap the Voice Memos app on the Home screen (it may be in the Utilities folder).
  • Tap the Lists button in the lower right corner.
  • Tap the desired recording reference to select it (e.g., “11:03 AM 11/15/11” in Figure 11-3).
  • Tap the recording reference again to play it. Tap the reference again to pause or stop the playback.
  • Tap the Speaker button in the upper left corner to playback over external speaker.

Seeing the time and date of the recordings isn’t all that helpful. It would be better if you could label the recording based on the purpose or content of the recording. Fortunately, you can do just that.

To Label a Recording

  • Tap the Voice Memos app on the Home screen (it may be in the Utilities folder).
  • Tap the Lists button in the lower right corner.
  • Tap the blue arrow to the right of the recording reference to see the recording Info screen (see Figure 11-4).
  • Tap the recording reference (e.g., “2:12 PM 00:09”) to bring up the Label screen.
  • Choose a label or tap the Custom option to enter your own unique label (e.g., “Dr. Lawler”). After entering a custom label, tap the Label icon in the upper left corner to return to the Label screen.
  • Tap the Info icon in the upper left corner to return to the Info screen and see your custom label (see Figure 11-5).
  • Tap the Voice Memos icon in the upper left corner to return to the list of voice memos.

In addition to labeling your recordings, you can also trim the recording, i.e., edit out extraneous material at the beginning or end.

To Trim a Recording

  • Tap the Voice Memos app on the Home screen (it may be in the Utilities folder).
  • Tap the Lists button in the lower right corner.
  • Tap the blue arrow to the right of the desired recording reference to see the recording Info screen.
  • Tap the Trim Memo option to bring up the editing/trimming screen (see Figure 11-6).
  • Drag the slider from the left side toward the center to trim the memo’s beginning and/or from the right side toward the center to trim the memo’s ending. Press the play button to play back the trimmed recording (see Figure 11-7). You can continue to adjust the slider on either end until you’re happy with the trim.
  • Tap Trim Voice Memo to finalize the edit.
  • Tap the Voice Memos icon in the upper left corner to return to the list of voice memos.
