Chapter 5: The Calendar App Overview

Creating Appointments

The Calendar app is used to enter appointments that are date- and time-specific. Examples of Calendar appointments include:

  • Lunch with Josie on Thursday at 1:00 PM
  • Veterinarian appointment for dog (Picket) on Monday at 3:45 PM
  • Teeth cleaning on Saturday at 10:15 AM
  • Pick up kids at mall on Wednesday at 7:00 PM

The Calendar app provides the opportunity to include the location of the appointment, the appointment's specific start and end times, a designated alarm (reminder) that the appointment is about to occur, and a place to enter notes related to the appointment. It is important to include as much information about the appointment as possible so tha you have a permanent record that can be easily accessed at any point in the future.

To Open the Calendar App

  • Tap the Calendar app on the Home screen.

Types of Calendar Appointments

The Calendar program can be used to create two types of appointments:

  1. One-time Appointments — One-time appointments are appointments that will occur only once, not on an ongoing or regular basis (e.g., lunch with Mike).
  2. Recurring Appointments — Recurring appointments are appointments that occur more than once and on a regular basis (e.g., a weekly meeting or an annual anniversary).

Calendar Views

When the Calendar app opens, it will display one of these possible calendar views:

  • List — provides a quick day, date, and time summary of all of your scheduled events
  • Day — provides a day-at-a-glance view of your day
  • Week — provides a week-at-a-glance view of your appointments (iPad only)
