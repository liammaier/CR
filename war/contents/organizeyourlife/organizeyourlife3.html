Wi-Fi

You will inevitably want to use Wi-Fi to access the Internet, send and receive e-mail, purchase and download apps, etc. from a home wireless network or when you are out and about.

To Turn on Wi-Fi and Establish a Connection

  • Tap the Settings app ( ) on the Home screen (it may be located in a Utilities folder).
  • Tap the Wi-Fi option.
  • Tap the Wi-Fi ON/OFF button at the top to turn Wi-Fi on. Available Wi-Fi networks will appear under the “Choose a Network” section of the screen. A lock to the left of the network indicates a passcode is needed to access that network (see Figure 3-2).
  • Tap the desired network. Enter any required information (e.g., passcode).
  • Tap Settings to return to the Settings screen or the Home button to return to the Home screen.

Changing the Wallpaper

You may want to change the background image or wallpaper of the Locked Screen or of the Home screen to personalize your device a bit.

To Change the Locked Screen or Home Screen Wallpaper

Rearranging Apps on the Home Screen(s)

We've mentioned that you can have more than one Home screen. But how do you create an additional Home screen and how do you move application icons from one location to another or from one Home screen to another?

Let's start with rearranging apps on a Home screen. For instance, you might want the apps you use the most at the top of your screen or placed in the Fixed Apps section of the Home screen.

To Rearrange Application Icons on Home Screen

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap and drag icons to rearrange apps. [Note: Be sure to give the other icons time to move out of the way before letting go of an icon; dropping one icon on top of another creates a folder.]
  • Press the Home button to save the new app icon arrangement (and stop the wiggling).
37Chapter 3: More iOS 5 Basics

We talked about additional Home screens and why you might create them for organizational purposes (e.g., keeping similarly-themed apps together). So not only can you rearrange app icons on a Home screen, icons can be moved from one Home screen to another. iOS 5 allows you to have up to 11 separate Home screens.

To Create Additional Home Screens

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap and drag an app icon to the right edge of the screen; a new Home screen will appear.
  • Press the Home button to save the change(s).

You learned about the Home Screen Page Reference section in Chapter 2. The Home Screen Reference is where you can orient yourself to the number of Home screens on your device and determine which Home screen you are currently looking at. Remember, the white circle indicates the current Home screen. Gray circles to the left and/or right indicate additional Home screens.

To Move Between Home Screens

  • Tap the current Home screen and slide (flick) to the right or left to move to the desired Home screen. For example, to move to a Home Screen to the right, flick from right to left.

To Move Application Icons to a Secondary Home Screen

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap and drag icon to the right or left edge to move app to different Home screen.
  • Press the Home button to save the new app arrangement.

To Eliminate Unwanted Home Screens

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen to be eliminated. The apps should begin to wiggle.
  • Tap and drag the apps on that Home screen to a different Home screen. Once there are no apps on the secondary Home screen, the screen and its page reference will disappear.

Creating Folders

Folders are yet another way you can organize the various apps you will accumulate over time. Utilizing folders allows you to create and name folders and then place similar apps within the folder for better organization (See Figure 3-9). You can include up to 12 apps in any folder. iOS 5 will attempt to name all new folders based on the type of app(s) placed in that folder (e.g., travel). Not to worry-you can always rename the folder so that it makes sense to you (e.g., notetaking, games, finance, etc.).

39Chapter 3: More iOS 5 Basics

To Create a New Folder on the Home Screen

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap and drag an app icon on top of another app icon until a new folder appears. For organizational purposes, it is best to place apps with a similar theme into the same folder.
  • To name the folder something other than the default name, tap the gray X to the right of the name field to clear the default folder name.
  • Enter the desired folder name. Be sure to name the folder something appropriate for that category of apps.
  • Press the Home button to save the changes.

Once apps are placed within a folder, simply tap the folder to view all the apps available. Tap the desired app to open it. Press the Home button to leave the app and return to the Home screen.

To Remove an App from a Folder

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap the desired folder to open it.
  • Tap and drag the icon for the to-be-removed app out of the folder and onto the Home screen.
  • Press the Home button to save the changes.

To Delete a Folder from a Home Screen

  • Tap and hold any icon on the Home screen until the apps begin to wiggle.
  • Tap the desired folder to open it.
  • Tap and drag each app icon from the folder to the Home screen until the folder disappears.
  • Press the Home button to save the changes.

  • Press the Home button to save the changes.
  • Remove the folder from the Home screen.
  • Press the Home button to save the changes.

Siri

Siri is a new addition to the Apple iPhone and is currently available only on the iPhone 4S. Siri is a voice recognition app that will impact not only how the iPhone is used in general, but also who can use an iPhone. For instance, some people may avoid using an iPhone because of the onscreen keyboard or because they have a hard time spelling. Siri will potentially change all that. Imagine saying, “Set alarm for 3:00 pm everyday” and having the device do just that. Siri will typically repeat what it hears you say and then provide a response and/or a question related to your commands (e.g., “Would you like to confirm the alarm?”).

Although this feature will make a huge difference in how we use the device, it isn't perfect. It is still important to learn the fundamental (non-Siri) way to do things on your device so that, when Siri doesn't quite get it right, you'll be able to swoop in and save the day!
