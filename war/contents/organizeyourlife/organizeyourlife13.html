Chapter 13: Camera & Photos Apps

Camera App

The camera can be a fun and very useful tool to help with memory problems. The camera can be used in lots of ways:

  • Take a picture of your friends or family at a party.
  • Take a picture of the row you parked your car in at the mall.
  • Take a video of your child’s ball game.
  • Take a picture of someone and assign it to a Contact.
  • Take a picture of which entrance to the mall you used.

When first opening the camera, you may be asked whether you would like to initiate Location services. Figure 13-1 shows the Location alert screen. Tap OK to allow your current location to be associated with photos taken or Don’t Allow to turn location tracking off.

Once you make your Location selections, the camera is ready to use.

Taking a Picture with the Camera

  • Tap the Camera icon on the Home Screen. The camera can also be accessed from the Locked screen. When the Locked screen is visible, double-tap the Home Button to bring up a camera icon next to the Unlock slider..
  • Respond to the Current Location Alert box (if one appears).
  • Point the camera at your target (see Figure 13-2). Keep the screen facing you so you can use it as a viewfinder.
  • Press the Camera button at the bottom middle of the screen OR press the device’s volume up button.

The Options button at the top middle of the screen provides the option to turn a Grid ON or OFF to help with framing your picture. Figure 13-3 shows the Options screen, while Figure 13-4 shows the Grid option ON.

Video

The Camera can also record video.

Recording Video with the Camera

  • Tap and slide the Camera/Video switch in the lower right corner of the Camera screen to the video icon on the right.
  • Point the camera at the desired target.
  • Press the Record button at the bottom middle of the screen OR press the device’s volume up button.
  • Press the button again to stop recording.

Photos App

The Photos app works independently, but it can also work in conjunction with the Camera app. You will notice that these two apps share some of the same features: Share, Slideshow, and Delete.

As mentioned previously, this section will discuss navigating among photos, creating albums, and editing photos.

Navigating Photos

Photos can be grouped into albums (discussed next). When viewing photos, slide or flick to the left (next photo) or right (previous photo) to move from photo to photo in the album. Tap the photo to view available photo options (see Figure 13-7).

220Organize Your Life Using iOS5

Three options appear at the bottom of the screen that we have discussed previously:

  • Share – Share your photos via e-mail, etc.
  • Slideshow – Set Slideshow options.
  • Trash – Delete a photo.

Photo Albums

The Photo app includes two albums by default:

  • Camera Roll
  • Photo Stream (when activated as part of iCloud)

Prior to iOS 5, additional photo albums could not be created on the device. Now, there is an easy way to organize/categorize your photos.

To Create Photo Albums

  • Tap the Photos app on the Home screen.
  • Navigate to the Albums screen (see Figure 13-8). If not already on the Albums screen, you will have to tap the icon in the upper left corner (e.g., Camera Roll, Albums) several times to get to the Albums screen.
  • Tap Edit in the upper right corner.
  • Tap Add in the upper left corner (see Figure 13-9).
  • Enter the name of the new photo album (see Figure 13-10).
  • Tap Save. Verify new album appears in Albums list (e.g., iOS 5). The new album will be empty until you move photos to that album (see Figure 13-11).
  • Tap Done.
