Chapter 18: Phone & FaceTime

This chapter discusses both the iPhone’s Phone app and FaceTime. Although FaceTime isn’t unique to iPhone users, I am combining the two in this chapter because they overlap.

Phone

This section of the chapter is for those of you who have an iPhone. Most apps and features discussed throughout this book work on all iOS 5 devices: iPhone, iPad, and iPod Touch. The iPhone is unique in that it can make and receive calls.

The Phone feature of the iPhone includes the following options (located as tabs at the bottom of the screen, as shown in Figure 18-1):

  • Favorites
  • Recents
  • Contacts
  • Keypad
  • Voicemail

Favorites

The Favorites tab allows you to identify “favorite” or frequently called contacts on one screen so you can quickly access their contact information and make calls. Figure 18-2 shows the Favorites screen. I have obscured the names of my contacts, but you should get the idea.

To Add Favorites to Your iPhone

  • Tap the Phone icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Favorites tab.
  • Tap the plus icon in the upper right corner.
  • Navigate to the contact to be added to your Favorites and tap it.
  • If necessary, select which phone number to use as the default (e.g., home, mobile, or work).
  • Tap Voice Call to add this contact’s phone number to your Favorites.

To Make a Call Using the Favorites Tab

  • Tap the Phone icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Favorites tab.
  • Tap the name of the contact to call the default number (e.g., home) or tap the blue arrow to the right of the contact name to open the Info screen and choose a different number (e.g., mobile).

To Delete a Contact from the Favorites Tab

  • Tap the Edit icon in the upper left corner.
  • Tap the red and white delete icon to the left of the contact’s name.
  • Tap Delete to the right of the contact’s name.

Recents

The Recents tab allows you to view All calls or Missed calls. The Recents call screen shows the name or number and the date of the call; it shows the time instead if the call came in on the current day. Figure 18-3 shows the Recents screen.

To Make a Call Using the Recents Tab

  • Tap the Recents tab.
  • Tap the name of the contact to call the number identified (e.g., home).

To Delete a Call from the Recents Tab

  • Tap Edit.
  • Tap the red and white delete icon to the left of the contact’s name or tap Clear to clear all recent calls.
  • Tap Delete to the right of the contact’s name.

Contacts

The Contacts tab brings up the Contacts screen as discussed previously.

Keypad

The Keypad tab is used to dial direct numbers or numbers not listed in your Contacts. Figure 18-4 shows the Keypad screen.

Voicemail

The Voicemail tab is used to access voicemail messages. Figure 18-5 shows the Voicemail screen.

To Listen to a Voicemail

  • Tap the Voicemail tab.
  • Tap the desired voicemail reference to listen to the message. Tap Speaker to listen to the message over the external speaker.

To Make a Call Using the Voicemail Tab

  • Tap the Voicemail tab.
  • Tap the desired voicemail reference and tap the Call Back button.

To Delete a Voicemail Message

  • Tap the Voicemail tab.
  • Tap the desired voicemail reference and tap the Delete button or tap on the reference and drag to the right to see a Delete button.

To Create a Voicemail Greeting

  • Tap the Voicemail tab.
  • Tap the Greeting tab in the upper left corner.
  • Tap the Default option to use a standard voicemail greeting.
    OR
  • Tap the Custom option.
  • Tap the Record button.
  • Tap the Stop button.
  • Tap the Play button to hear your recording.
  • Tap Save to save your selection.

FaceTime

FaceTime is video calling where the people on the call can both see and hear each other. FaceTime calls can be made to anyone with a Mac or iOS 5 device with a camera.

To Make a FaceTime Call

  • Tap the Contacts icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap desired contact name.
  • Tap FaceTime.
  • Tap the phone number or e-mail of the contact.
