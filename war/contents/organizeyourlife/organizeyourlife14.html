Chapter 14: Maps & Safari

Maps and Safari are standard apps on iOS 5 devices; however, the apps require either 3G or Wi-Fi access to work.

Maps

The Maps app allows you to pinpoint your current position and get directions to any address entered. Figure 14-1 shows the Maps Search screen. From the Search screen, you can:

  • Enter an address directly into the Search field.
  • Tap the Contacts icon to the right of the Search field to use an address associated with a contact.
  • Enter the name of a business in the Search field (e.g., Starbucks or Coffee Bean).

To Conduct a Maps Search

  • Tap the Maps app icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Current Location icon in the lower left corner to set your current location.
  • Tap the Search button at the bottom of the screen.
  • Tap the Search field at the top of the screen to place the cursor there.
  • Enter the desired address, select a contact, or enter a search term.
  • Tap Search in the lower right corner.

Figure 14-2 shows the Search results when a search term (“Starbucks” in this example) is entered into the Search field. The blue dot indicates your current location, while the red dots represent the Starbucks locations nearby.

Tap a red dot to select that specific location. Tap the blue arrow to the right of the “Starbucks” pop-up to bring up the Info screen for that selection (see Figure 14-3).

To Get Written Directions (Verbal plus Map)

  • From the Info screen, tap either the Directions To Here or Directions From Here option.
  • Verify the current location in the Start field and the destination location in the Ends field.
  • Choose the desired mode of transportation at the top of the screen (i.e., car, bus, or walking).
  • Tap Route in the upper right corner.
  • Tap Start in upper right corner.
  • Tap Next (right) arrow in upper right corner to see the next step in the directions.

Figures 14-4, 14-5, and 14-6 show the Directions screens for auto, bus, and walking routes, respectively. The directions include the estimated time required for the trip, the total distance of the route, and the bus times and cost, when applicable.

Siri Basics

Siri allows you to find locations and get directions by speaking. To interact with the Maps app using Siri:

  1. Press and hold the Home Button until “What can I help you with?” and the microphone appear on the screen. Tap the microphone to turn it on if it is not blinking.
  2. Siri will understand a number of verbal commands, such as:
    1. Find Starbucks.
    2. Take me home.

Safari

Safari is the Web browser that comes with iOS 5. Safari can be used to access Web sites, search for Web content, save and access bookmarks, open multiple browser windows, etc. Figure 14-8 shows a typical Safari window with a Web site entered into the Address bar at the top of the screen.

Several other things to note on the Safari screen include:

  • Google Search field (upper right corner) – Use this field to enter search terms into the Google Search Engine.
  • Previous/Next Arrows (lower left corner) – Use the arrows to navigate to previously viewed pages and then forward through the sequence again.

To Open Multiple Web Pages

  • Tap the Safari app icon on the Home screen.
  • Tap the Page Reference button in the lower right corner of main Safari window.
  • Tap the New Page button in lower left corner.
  • Enter URL for Web page in Address bar.
  • Tap Go in the lower right corner.
  • To navigate among open Web pages, do one of the following:
    * Use the right and left arrows in the lower left corner.
    OR
    * Tap the Page Reference button, flick right or left until you see the page youwant, then tap that page.
