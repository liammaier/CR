The Reminder's App

The Reminders app is new to iOS 5 and can be used to keep a simple task list or list of things you need to do or purchase, etc. You can set a reminder for a task on a particular day and date. In addition, you can be reminded about items based on your location, e.g., as you leave a location or as you are approaching a location.

The Reminders app includes two lists by default:

  • Reminders (see Figure 9-1)
  • Completed (see Figure 9-2)

The initial Reminders list is blank until you begin to add items to it. All items completed (checked off), regardless of the specific list they appear on, will be moved to the Completed list.

Reminders Settings

There are two settings that can be changed within the Reminders section.

  • Sync
  • Default List

Sync

The Sync setting allows you to set how far back in time you would like to sync Reminders. The options are:

  • Reminders 2 Weeks Back
  • Reminders 1 Month Back (default setting)
  • Reminders 3 Months Back
176Organize Your Life Using iOS5
  • Reminders 6 Months Back
  • All Reminders

It might seem unnecessary to sync reminders more than one month back; however, if your purpose is to use the device as a memory prosthetic, you will want to choose All Reminders. Think about it; if you only sync reminders from one month back, any reminder prior to one month will not appear on your device. It will still appear on iCloud, but it won’t appear on your device. Ask yourself the question, “Which device will I tend to have available at any time or place when I need to see if and when I completed a particular task?”

To Change the Sync Setting

  • Tap Settings from the Home screen.
  • Tap Mail, Contacts, Calendars.
  • Scroll to the Reminders section.
  • Tap the Sync option.
  • Select the desired sync option (All Reminders is strongly recommended).
  • Tap the Mail… icon in the upper left corner to return to the Mail, Contacts, Calendar screen.

Default List

The Reminders app works on a variety of cognitive skills simultaneously. It’s much like having a list of things to do on a sheet of paper, but better! Many of the skills discussed previously play a role in using the Reminders app. For instance, it is important that any alerts you set give you ample time. In addition, you need to make sure that the title of the reminder is detailed enough so you know exactly what you need to do or where you need to go when you get to that list item.

Have you ever left the house to go to the store and forgotten your list of things to buy? Have you ever been sure that you brought the list with you, but can’t find it in your purse/wallet or car? Your smart device allows you to keep all your important information in one place. You won’t lose some scrap of paper you wrote your list on, because now that list will be on your device and you’ll be carrying it with you all the time.
