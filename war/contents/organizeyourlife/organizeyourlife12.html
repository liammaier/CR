Mail

Mail allows you to send and receive e-mail via your device. This chapter will briefly discuss the Mail app. However, e-mail is a huge topic; therefore, the intent here is just to get you started, rather than to provide a comprehensive overview of the Mail app.

See the Mail Settings section of this chapter if you do not have e-mail set up on your device yet. Alternatively, your service provider can help you set up an e-mail account.

To Open the Mail App

  • Tap the Mail icon on the Home screen.

On the Message screen, there are several options available (besides just reading the message, that is). Options appear at the bottom of the screen on the iPhone and iPod Touch and in the top right corner on the iPad:

  • Refresh – This option allows you to refresh the screen.
  • Move – This option allows you to move the message to a different mailbox or folder other than the Inbox (e.g., Drafts, Sent, Trash, etc.). To access a particular mailbox or folder, tap the Mailboxes icon at the top left of the Inbox screen.
  • Trash – Trash allows you to delete the message.
  • Reply/Forward – This option allows you to reply, forward, or print the message. Printing from your device requires a special printer feature.
  • Compose – Compose allows you to create a new e-mail message.

When done with the message, return to the Inbox by tapping the Inbox icon at the top left of the screen.

To Create a New E-mail Message

  • Tap the Compose icon in the lower right corner (upper right corner on iPad).
  • Enter appropriate information in the To and Cc/Bcc fields. Tap the plus sign to the right of the field to look up an e-mail address in the Contacts app.
  • Enter appropriate information in the Subject field and the body section of the message.
  • Tap Send to send the message.

To Delete an E-mail Message

  • From the list of messages, tap and drag from left to right over the message listing until the Delete icon appears.
  • Tap Delete.
    OR
  • Tap the e-mail listing to open the message.
  • Tap the Trash icon at the bottom middle of the screen. The e-mail will be moved to the Trash folder immediately; no confirmation message will appear.

You may have noticed an Edit icon in the upper right corner of the Inbox. This allows you to quickly Delete, Move, and Mark a message without having to actually open the message.

To Delete, Mark, or Move E-mail Messages

  • Tap the Edit icon in the top right of the Inbox screen.
  • Select the desired messages (more than one message can be selected).
  • Tap the desired action: Delete, Move, or Mark. The Mark option allows you to flag a message and/or mark it as unread.

Mail Settings

New Mail accounts are created via Settings. In addition to creating new Mail accounts, there are several Mail settings that can be set via Settings.

To Create a New Mail Account

  • Tap Settings from the Home screen.
  • Tap Mail, Contacts, Calendars.
  • Tap Add Account… from the Accounts section (see Figure 12-3).
  • Tap the mail account type (e.g., iCloud, Gmail, Yahoo!, Microsoft Exchange).
  • Enter the requested mail account information (you may need to get this info from your service provider).
  • Tap the Mail… icon in the upper left corner to return to the Mail, Contacts, Calendar screen.

Siri Basics

Siri allows you to create and search mail messages by speaking. To interact with the Mail app using Siri:

  1. Press and hold the Home Button until “What can I help you with?” and the microphone appear on the screen. Tap the microphone to turn it on if it is not blinking.
  2. Siri will understand a number of verbal commands, such as:
    1. E-mail Ed about lunch.
    2. Search for message from Barbara Hall.

Messages

Messaging (often called texting) is something that was previously only available on iPhones with a texting plan through your wireless phone company. iOS 5 allows individuals to text SMS (short message service) and MMS (multimedia messaging service) messages to other iOS 5 devices free of charge. Two important caveats apply here:

  • In order to take advantage of this “freebie” on an iPad or iPod Touch, you must use the e-mail address associated with the recipient’s Apple ID (check with him or her regarding which address to use).
  • Sending messages from your iPhone to a non-iOS 5 device may involve a charge, and charges can really add up, so be sure you know what is and isn’t included in the data plan from your provider.

It is worth mentioning that texting while driving is illegal in many states and, in any case, is not a safe practice!

To Compose a New Message

  • Tap the Messages icon on the Home screen.
  • If a screen other than the Messages screen appears, tap the Messages icon in the upper left corner to get to the Messages screen.
  • Tap the Compose icon in the upper right corner.
  • Enter the recipient’s name after the To: prompt or tap the plus icon to view your Contacts (see Figure 12-6) and select the desired contact. If your contact has both phone and e-mail information listed, select the phone number.
  • Tap and enter your message in the small field just above the keyboard and left of the Send button.
  • (Optional) Tap the camera icon to take a picture and send it as part of the message.
  • Tap Send when the message is complete.

Below is a brief discussion of the options shown above.
