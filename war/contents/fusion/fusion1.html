Warm Up

5 minutes

Count to 10; Count on from 2, 3, 4 with a number line

Materials: Number line (folded in half so that only 1-10 is visible)

  • Everyone, count to 10. Signal (clap, tap, or snap) so students stay together.
  • Call on individual students to check that each can count to 10.
  • If an error occurs, provide corrective feedback and have the student count again.
  • Show number line.
    • Now, you will count to 10 again, but this time, you will start at different numbers.
    • Touch the number 2.
    • Everyone, count to 10 starting with 2. Get ready.

Activity 1

Identify numbers 1-5

Materials: Number cards 1-5

  • Show number cards 1-5 in random order and have the group identify the numbres.
  • What number?
CORRECT RESPONSE STUDENT ERRORS
Yes, 4. Place correctly identified number on the table. This number is 4. What number? (4)
Yes, 4. Keep number card for another turn.
  • Repeat until all the number cards have been correctly identified.
  • Lightning Round: Give each student a turn to identify the numbers quickly. Place correctly identified numbers on table. Correct and keep numbers incorrectly identified for another turn.

Activity 2

Make models equal to numbers 1-5

Materials: 5 sets of 5 connecting cubes each (each set a different color)

Vocabulary: Same

  • Pass out a number card to each student.
    • To yourself, count out cubes to match your number, then connect the cubes to make a train. Provide work time.
  • Ask each student:

Activity 3

Identify models and tell how many

Materials: Cube trains from previous activity

  • Collect the different cube trains and spread them out in front of the group in random order.
    • Everyone, point to the train that has 2. Repeat with 5, 1, 4, and 3.
CORRECT RESPONSE STUDENT ERRORS
Yes, this train has 2. Touch each cube as you count, 1, 2. Let's see if this train has 2. Touch and count, 1, 2, 3.
No, this train does not have 2.
Touch the correct train as you say, Do you think this train has 2? Touch and count, 1, 2. Yes, this train has 2.
  • Give each student an individual turn, having them point to one of the trains you name.
  • Touch one of the trains and ask students to tell you how many.
    • Now it's your turn to tell me how many there are. How many?
  • Give each student an individual turn having them tell how many are in the train you point to.

Activity 4

Sequence models 1-5

Materials: Cube trains from previous activity

Vocabulary: Least, smallest, first, next, after, last

    • Now let's put the trains in order starting with the train with the least or smallest number of the cubes. Point to the train that should go first.
    • Why does this train go first?

  • Allow students to share. Possible answer: the train with only 1 cube has the least or smallest numbers of cubes.

    • Place that train in front of the group
    • I wonder which train goes next. Which train goes after the train of 1? Who has an idea?

  • Allow students to share. Possible answers: the train of 2 goes next because it is the next number after 1; it is one more than 1; it is the next largest.

  • Confirm correct responses and have that student place that train next to the previous train.
  • Continue asking Which train goes next? Why? Give each student a turn. Continue to place each train in the sequence. After 4 trains are placed:
    • Which train goes last in the sequence?
  • Call on a student to tell why this train goes last and place it last in the sequence.
    • Let's check out trains to see if they are in order. Count as I touch each train. (1, 2, 3, 4, 5)
    • Great job! You put all the trains in order.

Activity 5

Identify models that are more

Materials: Cube trains from previous activity

Vocabulary: More, longer

    • Now, I'm going to show you two trains and you're going to point to the train that has more.
  • Place a train of 5 cubes and a train of 2 cubes in front of the group.

    • Look at these two trains. Think in your head which train has more.
  • Provide think time and monitor as the students look at both choices.
    • Now, point to the train that has more.
  • Confirm correct responses and call on a student who answered correctly.
    • How do you know this train has more?

  • Possible answers: I counted (or saw) that this train has 5 and this one has 2, and 5 is more; this train is longer so it has more.

  • Reword to confirm:
    • This train has 5 cubes and this one has 2 cubes, so the train with 5 has more.
      That's one way to tell.
    • This train is longer so you know that it has more. That's one way to tell.
    • After one of the above ideas is offered, ask, Does anyone have another idea of how we can tell which train has more?

  • Possible answer: the two trains can be compared 1:1 to see which has more.

  • This is similar to telling which is longer, but requires a side-by-side comparison, matching each cube and seeing that there is more in the longer train. If students don't offer this idea, show them.
  • Repeat with cube trains for 1 and 3.

Activity 6

Identify models that are less

Materials: Cube trains from previous activity

Vocabulary: Less, shorter (or smaller)

    • I'm going to show you two trains and you're going to point to the train that has less.
  • Place a train of 1 cube and a train of 4 cubes in front of the group.
    • Look at these two trains. Think in your head which train has less.
  • Provide think time and monitor as students look at both choices.

Wrap Up

Number writing

Materials: Number writing worksheets

  • If time allows, have students gain accuracy (legibility) and fluency in writing numbers using these worksheets. Begin with tracing and writing numbers 0-10 to see how legibly students write numbers. Individualize practice with subsequent worksheets if a student has difficulty with a particular number.
  • Use this activity if you finish early and students need additional number writing practice. During this time, you can also provide additional practice to those students who may have experienced difficulties with particular parts of the lesson.
  • Students should continue number writing practice until written fact timings begin in later lessons, by which time they should be fluent and accurate in writing numbers.
